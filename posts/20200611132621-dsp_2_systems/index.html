DSP 2: Continuous-time and Discrete-time Systems

Tests

Continuous time system, s-domain. With the basis in physics, allowing physical intuition that aids understanding.

Discrete time system z-domain. Allow fast and convenient implementation for digital computational systems.

Conversion with bilinear transform.

Frequency response of continuous time system G(s) is basically G(jw) where w is the angular velocity in radian per second.

Frequency response of discrete time system H(z) is different, as there is no notion of seconds,

H(ejw), where 0 <= w <= 2pi

If we transfer that to the continuous time, we can consider a sinuisoidal signal sin(wt), in a single time step it’s moving a discrete phase of w*dt
