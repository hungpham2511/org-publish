Transforming notes to blog posts

June 12, 2020
Table of Contents
  • Why?
  • Solution
  • Citations

Why?

Keeping lots of notes I keep a lots of notes with many different ideas and thoughts, but they are not well maintained. As a result, they are not very useful for myself or for others. Turning some of these notes into blog posts can be useful.

Also publish writings is a great way to force one to polish the skill, to focus on making concise ideas and to improve the clarity of one’s own ideas.

However, the blog posts I am working on is somewhat detached from my own org system. It’s this system that contains the knowledge. It’s only natural to have the blog posts to be another view of the knowledge base, or the main system. Put it this way, the blog posts are basically outlet of notes that I am happy about. Period. Nothing more and certainly nothing less.

Solution

The solution is a way to publish new blog posts directly from the org notes. Put it in another way, I need to publish my org notes.

I use ox-hugo to generate new blog posts in markdown. For each org note that is to be published, fill in the front matter When satisfied with the note, use `org-export` (C-c C-e H h) to generate the markdown post.

#+TITLE: Transforming note to blog posts
#+HUGO_SECTION: posts
#+HUGO_CATEGORIES: blog hugo
#+HUGO_BASE_DIR: publish
#+DATE: 2020-06-12
#+toc: headlines 2

To preview the site

cd ~/org/publish && hugo serve

To build and deploy the site

cd ~/org/publish && ./deploy.sh
